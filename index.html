    METABOLIC DOCTRINE — DEFICIT LINE PROTOCOL

    ΔE = Energyin − Energyout

    Holding a controlled negative ΔE — intake pinned deliberately below expenditure — forces the system to close the energy gap from stored substrate, shifting fuel selection toward lipid oxidation while sparing lean mass behind the 180g protein floor. Because intake arrives as protein-forward, low-glycemic loads, blood glucose holds a flat, stable band instead of spiking and crashing; stable glucose means no reactive-hypoglycemia fog, steadier catecholamine tone, and a clean, sustained supply of energy to both working muscle and the prefrontal cortex. The result is a compounding edge: fat mass trends down on the deficit line while training output, focus depth, and decision quality stay flat-to-up — a controlled delta, not a crash.
